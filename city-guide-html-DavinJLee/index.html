The following is a city guide covering the major attractions, hotels, and bars of Chicago, Illinois. We're glad you're here!

Chicago is one of the United States' most culturally prominent cities. 2,746,388 people live in the city today, and in the past it's been called home by a whole host of famous names, from Lady Gaga and Kanye West to Barack Obama and Al Capone! In 2023 the city made over 19 billion dollars in revenue from tourism alone.

What is it about Chicago that captivates the attention of all these millions of people? The best way to find out is to visit yourself! Activities

Our favorite things to do in Chicago!

Walk the Riverside

The Riverside is one of America's most iconic urban walks. Stroll along the Chicago River in the shadow of the skyscrapers or take a boat for one of the world-renowned architecture tours! A map below shows the start of the path on foot, but we've left out any pictures for this one. Wouldn't want to spoil the experience, after all.

Willis Tower

Willis Tower, once Sears Tower, is a local landmark that soars 1,451 feet into the Chicago skyline. Its peak can be seen from 4 different states! After opening in 1973, its name was changed in 2009 with the corporate downfall of Sears. Its offices are occupied by several prominent US companies, including American Airlines, Willis Towers Watson, and Morgan Stanley. The view from the top floors, called the Skydeck, is one of Chicago's most breathtaking. To find out more and reserve your tickets, click the link to the website here~!

Hotels

Sleep and stay in style!

Waldorf Astoria

Perfect to return to after a long day of sightseeing, the Waldorf Astoria is a luxury hotel in the stylish center of Chicago. Rooms start at $813, and guests can enjoy a variety of perks ranging from an indoor pool to a robust fitness center.

Fast Facts!

  • The spa features a retail boutique and a heated lap pool and is over 14,000 square feet in size.
  • The Parisian style event venues are popular choices for weddings and formal events.
  • Not one, not two, but three different cafes call the Waldorf Astoria home. Room service is 24-hours!

Freehand Chicago

For the more budget constrained, the Freehand Chicago is a downtown-based hotel with rooms starting at around $125 in price. Enjoy all the benefits of the centralized location without breaking the bank! This is a 3-star hotel with a 4-star review on Google. The building itself is an authentic piece of Chicago history, dating back to 1927.
